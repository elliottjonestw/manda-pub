How it Works

Your Data

Where your data is stored, how syncing works, and how to keep it safe.

Manda is local-first: your study data lives in your own browser, on your own device, and it belongs to you. Nothing you do (the words you save, the texts you read, your review history) is sent anywhere unless you choose to sign in and sync. This page explains where that data is kept, and how to make sure you never lose it.

Where your data is stored

Your data is saved in IndexedDB, a database built into every modern browser. When you open Manda, everything is loaded into memory once so the app can read it instantly; as you study, changes are written back to IndexedDB in the background within milliseconds.

  • If IndexedDB isn't available (some private-browsing modes block it), Manda falls back to localStorage, and as a last resort keeps your data in memory for the current session.
  • Manda asks the browser to mark its storage as persistent, which reduces the chance the browser clears it automatically to free up space.
  • One small exception: your light/dark theme preference is kept in localStorage so the right theme can be painted instantly, before the rest of the app has loaded.

What's stored

Everything that makes the app yours: your Study List and Known Words, your review schedule and study stats, your saved texts and reading history, word notes, Learning Path progress, and your reader settings.

Syncing across devices

Because the data is tied to one browser, it doesn't automatically appear on your other devices. To carry your progress between, say, your laptop and your phone, create a free account and sign in on the Account page. From there:

  • Sync to Cloud uploads a copy of your learning data to your account.
  • Restore from Cloud pulls that copy back down onto whichever device you're signed in to.

Only your actual learning data is synced; transient things like the current reader session are left out. Sync always reads the latest data straight from your browser's database at the moment you sync, so having Manda open in another tab won't cause it to upload stale data.

Backing up to a file

You don't need an account to keep a safety copy. On the Import/Export Data page you can export a single backup file containing all of your data, then import it later, on the same device or a new one. You can also export your vocabulary as an Anki deck to study elsewhere.

Keeping your data safe

Since your data lives in the browser, it can be lost the same ways any browser data can: clearing your browsing data or site data, uninstalling the browser, or closing a private/incognito window can all remove it. To be safe, do one or both of these:

  • Sign in and Sync to Cloud, so a copy lives in your account.
  • Periodically export a backup file from the Import/Export Data page.
